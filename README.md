Detailed Task Summary:

Task 1:
I created a one-page Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic in landscape mode using Canva. The poster is designed in a simple and colourful style so that first-year students can understand it quickly. It explains five important topics with clear icons and bullet points: what digital literacy actually means, useful digital tools for students, safe internet practices, how to build a professional online presence, and basic email etiquette. I used bright blue and green colours to make it attractive and kept the layout clean with numbered sections. The most interesting part for me was arranging all the information visually so it becomes easy to remember. The final infographic is saved in the task-1-presentation folder.

Task 2:
I built my student digital portfolio by setting up professional accounts on three important platforms. On GitHub I created a special profile README repository with my full name, branch, year and one sentence about what I hope to learn in the coming four years. On LinkedIn I properly filled the Education section mentioning VIT Bhopal University, B.Tech degree and expected graduation year. On Kaggle I created a basic profile that I can use for future data science and machine learning practice. Each platform has its own purpose: GitHub for projects and coding portfolio, LinkedIn for professional networking and internships, and Kaggle for skill practice. In the next four years I plan to regularly update these profiles and upload small projects. Screenshots of all three profiles are saved in the task-2-portfolio folder.

Task 3:
I completed a beginner-level coding challenge called “Solve Me First” on the HackerRank platform and took a screenshot of the successful submission. After that I created a Google Form named “Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz” containing exactly 5 questions including one multiple choice question and one short answer question. This form can be used to test my batchmates’ knowledge about digital literacy. I also took screenshots of the form itself and the linked Google Sheet where responses will appear. These tools are very useful for academic work because HackerRank helps improve coding skills and Google Forms makes it easy to create quizzes and collect feedback from many students at once. All screenshots and files are saved in the task-3-platforms folder.

Task 4:
I drafted two professional emails following all standard email etiquette rules. The first email is a polite request to a professor for a short extension on an assignment submission. The second email is written to an internship coordinator expressing genuine interest in a summer internship opportunity. Both emails have a clear subject line, professional greeting, well-structured body and proper sign-off. In addition, I created a Social Media Do’s and Don’ts checklist containing 5 Do’s and 5 Don’ts specially for college students. This checklist helps students use social media responsibly. All three files (two emails and the checklist) are saved in the task-4-email-etiquette folder.

Task 5:
I prepared a detailed case study on the cybercrime “Phishing”. In the case study I explained what phishing is, how it typically happens step-by-step, who is usually targeted and what the possible consequences are. I also created a “Stay Safe Online” prevention checklist with 8 practical tips for college students in India. At least two tips are related to UPI and financial safety. The checklist also includes the official reporting channel – National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and Helpline number 1930. While doing this task I learned many new things about online safety. Both the case study and the prevention checklist files are saved in the task-5-cybercrime folder.
